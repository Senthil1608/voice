DISEASES

GERD(Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease):

  • Persistent Heartburn: GERD causes frequent and persistent heartburn due to stomach acid backing up into the esophagus.
  • Esophageal Damage: Long-term acid exposure can lead to inflammation, ulcers, strictures, and Barrett's esophagus, increasing cancer risk.
  • Treatment Options: Lifestyle changes, medication (such as PPIs and H2 blockers), and surgery are common approaches to manage GERD and prevent complications.





Voice Nodule

  • Hoarseness and Strain: Voice nodules cause persistent hoarseness and vocal strain, often resulting from vocal cord overuse or misuse.
  • Vocal Cord Damage: Prolonged strain can lead to the formation of nodules, callous-like growths on the vocal cords, impairing voice quality and function.
  • Treatment Approaches: Treatment typically involves voice therapy to correct vocal habits and reduce strain, along with rest and hydration. In severe cases, surgical removal may be necessary to alleviate symptoms and restore vocal health.


Tonsillitis:

  • Sore Throat and Difficulty Swallowing: Tonsillitis is characterized by a sore throat, often accompanied by pain or difficulty swallowing.
  • Swollen Tonsils: The tonsils, located at the back of the throat, become inflamed and swollen due to viral or bacterial infections.
  • Treatment Options: Treatment may include rest, fluids, pain relievers, and antibiotics in bacterial cases. Severe or recurrent tonsillitis may require surgical removal of the tonsils (tonsillectomy) to alleviate symptoms and prevent further infections.


Vocal Fold Edema:

  • Symptoms: Vocal fold edema manifests as hoarseness, difficulty speaking, and throat discomfort.
  • Causes: It results from vocal strain, infections, allergies, or irritants like smoking and acid reflux.
  • Treatment : Treatments include vocal rest, hydration, and medications to reduce inflammation, with surgery in severe cases.
