GERD:
  • Causes: Weakened lower esophageal sphincter, lifestyle choices, obesity, certain medical conditions.
  • Symptoms: Acid reflux, heartburn, regurgitation.
  • Complications: Barrett's esophagus, esophageal ulcers.
Vocal Nodule:
  • Risk Factors: Voice strain, misuse, extensive voice use.
  • Symptoms: Hoarseness, vocal fatigue, pitch changes.
  • Prevention: Vocal rest, proper hydration, voice therapy.
Tonsillitis:
  • Types: Viral and bacterial.
  • Symptoms: Sore throat, difficulty swallowing, swollen tonsils.
  • Treatment: Antibiotics (for bacterial), rest, pain relief.
Vocal Fold Edema:
  • Causes: Inflammation, vocal overuse.
  • Symptoms: Voice fatigue, difficulty speaking.
  • Treatment: Voice rest, hydration, anti-inflammatory medications.
Vocal Polyps:
  • Formation: Due to vocal abuse, trauma.
  • Types: Vary in size and shape.
  • Treatment: Voice therapy, surgical removal.
Vocal Fold Paralysis:
  • Causes: Nerve damage, injury, infections.
  • Symptoms: Hoarseness, breathiness, difficulty swallowing.
  • Treatment: Voice therapy, surgery in severe cases.
Neck Mass (Tumour):
  • Evaluation: Range from benign to malignant.
  • Symptoms: Lump in the neck, pain, difficulty swallowing.
  • Diagnosis: Imaging, biopsy.
Sulcus Vocalis:
  • Characteristic: Groove or furrow on vocal folds.
  • Effects: Affects vocal fold structure and function.
  • Treatment: Voice therapy, surgical intervention in severe cases.
